Pocket Card

"I've got it from here."
I am not 11. I have the arsenal. The alarm is not the truth.

The anxious forecast is a hypothesis, not a verdict. Ask for evidence. Run small experiments on the fear — that's what a scientist does with a noisy signal.

✦

When the storm-brain fires

  1. Name it. One sentence. No adjectives.
  2. Ask it. What's the evidence? What are the real odds?
  3. Choose one:
    • Thin evidence → label "old alarm," release, return to the task.
    • Real evidence → write 3 prep steps. Do step 1 today.
  4. Log one good thing. One sentence. Every day.

Set it down, gently

  • The word "stupid." It's not stupid; it's an old alarm.
  • The identity of the one who barely escaped. One day, not a life sentence.
  • Pre-living disasters with tiny probability. Rehearsing doom is a tax, not preparation.
  • Comparing every unknown to the Balrog. Most new things are just Tuesdays.

The lines that hold

  • First arrow: the fact.
  • Second arrow: the story. Drop it.
  • "Thanks, 11-year-old me. I've got it from here."
  • My life has meaning and hard-won glory.
  • I refuse to open my arms to oblivion.

Tonight, one question

Did the feared thing happen? Did I handle what did?

— Accipio. Ludo. —
