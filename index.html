When the storm-brain fires

  1. Name it. One sentence. No adjectives.
  2. Ask it. What's the evidence? What are the real odds?
  3. Choose one:
    • Thin evidence → label "old alarm," release, return to the task.
    • Real evidence → write 3 prep steps. Do step 1 today.
  4. Log one good thing. One sentence. Every day.

The lines that hold

  • First arrow: the fact.
  • Second arrow: the story. Drop it.
  • "Thanks, 11-year-old me. I've got it from here."
  • My life has meaning and hard-won glory.
  • I refuse to open my arms to oblivion.
— Accipio. Ludo. —
